    AppendixMethodology & sources

    Source data. Every figure on this site comes from Tom Leatherwood's campaign financial disclosure reports filed with the Tennessee Registry of Election Finance (committee ID 7320), exported directly from the state's public database at apps.tn.gov/tncamp. Thirty reports, covering 3rd Quarter 2018 through 1st Quarter 2026 — the complete filing history, with every report's donor detail itemized on this page. The ledger reconciles: every report's ending balance matches the next report's beginning balance, and the career math closes to the penny.

    Classification. Contributions are bucketed using TREF's own contributor flags from the filings: "P" designates a PAC, "C" designates a candidate committee. Business entities without a flag (LLCs, corporations, funds) are grouped with special interests. Campaign committees and sitting elected officials donating personally are grouped with politicians. Everyone else is an individual. All 390 checks are listed above so any single classification can be checked.

    District status. Individual donors are matched to House District 99 by ZIP code from their own filing entries. "In District" is 38002 (Arlington/Lakeland), 38053 and 38054 (Millington), and 38014 (Brunswick). Donors in ZIPs split by the district boundary (38016, 38028, 38133, 38135) were resolved individually by street address against the current district map. Everyone else is "Out of District." PACs are never counted as in-district, even when one lists a district address — one PAC (Steamfitters Local 614) files from an Arlington address and is counted with special interests, since a PAC's mailbox is not a constituent.

    Election cycles and the "100%" claim. TREF assigns each report to an election cycle; supplemental reports filed in odd years belong to the following election's cycle. The chart's final data point and the 100% figure cover his three most recent filings — the two 2025 supplementals and the 1st Quarter 2026 report — spanning January 1, 2025 through the end of March 2026. The 2026 cycle is still underway; this page will be updated as he files new reports, including the 2nd Quarter and Pre-Primary reports due in July 2026.

    Corrections. Found an error? Every claim traces to a specific filing. Pull the report, check the math, and if something's wrong, it gets fixed.
